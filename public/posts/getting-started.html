Building Products with AI

Published on January 30, 2025

"You need three things to create a successful startup: to start with good people, to make something customers actually want, and to spend as little money as possible." - Paul Graham

AI drives productivity. Soon, we’ll see billion-dollar companies led by just a CEO, CTO, and CFO. AI bridges the gap from idea to execution. Paul Graham says success comes from being frugal, working with passionate people—whom he calls "animals"—and building things customers truly want. The real challenge is figuring out what people actually want.

Ideas often stem from pain—a problem or need you encounter, whether at work or in your personal life. Solving work problems is more lucrative than life problems as businesses spend money more than consumers.

Cloning a successful app is another way to generate ideas. Can you create an open-source version? Can you make it cheaper, offer it in a new geography, or apply it to a different domain or niche? Maybe add a new use case? These are all strong ways to start.

AI Models Are Like Web Browsers

AI Models are analogous to web browsers. There isn’t just one browser—there’s Chrome, Firefox, Brave, Tor, and Safari. Each serves different users with unique needs. Some focus on privacy, others on speed, security, or niche use cases. The same will happen with AI. There won’t be just one dominant AI model—there will be different flavors. Some will serve specific industries, some will cater to different geographies, others will introduce better use cases, new pricing models, or even open-source alternatives.

I am building SyntextAI to challenge myself and see if I could create something thousands of people would use. It started with a simple question: how can I make research easier, faster, and more accessible? Perplexity and Deepnote are already doing their part, but there’s room for more. SyntextAI could be an alternative—a tool built for niche markets, supporting more file types, and offering flexibility where others don’t.

And we’re thinking beyond just one region. SyntextAI is designed with global reach in mind. We want people everywhere to have the tools they need, tailored to their local language, regulations, and industry standards.

Building the SyntextAI MVP: Speed, Simplicity, and Scalability

When building an MVP, speed matters. The goal is to test an idea quickly, see if users engage with it, and iterate based on real feedback. I decided to integrate existing technologies to accelerate the rollout of SyntextAI, focusing on getting a functional prototype into users' hands as fast as possible.

Here’s how I structured the MVP:

1. Landing Page with Google Authentication

First impressions count. The landing page is the first thing users see, so it needed to be clean, simple, and functional. Instead of a lengthy sign-up process, I implemented Google Authentication, allowing users to sign in with a single click. This not only removes friction but also improves security by leveraging Google’s authentication framework.

2. Core App Functionality

The heart of the MVP is the app’s ability to process, analyze, and extract insights from documents. Rather than over-engineering the initial version, I focused on the core research assistant functionality—uploading files, parsing content, and delivering structured responses. The backend was kept simple but efficient, ensuring that users could start getting value immediately without a steep learning curve.

3. Settings Page with Stripe Integration

Monetization had to be built in from day one. Many great ideas fail because they lack a clear business model. To test whether people would pay for SyntextAI, I integrated Stripe for handling subscriptions and payments. The settings page allows users to manage their account, view their subscription status, and easily upgrade if they need additional features.

Why This Approach?

Instead of spending months on a perfect, fully polished product, I wanted to validate the demand first. By leveraging Google Auth and Stripe, I was able to focus on what truly matters—delivering a useful AI-powered research tool that people could actually use.

With the MVP live, the next step is gathering feedback, refining the user experience, and expanding features based on real user needs. The key to launching a successful AI product isn’t perfection—it’s rapid iteration, real-world testing, and solving actual problems.
