About Me

My Journey: From Curiosity to Code

It all kicked off in 1997 when a clunky PC landed in my house. What started with tinkering in spreadsheets and databases turned into a full-on obsession with cracking problems through tech. High school introduced me to Pascal, and I realized coding wasn’t just a tool, it was my way of attacking challenges head-on.

University locked it in. I started in Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering but jumped to Computer Systems Engineering because I craved building stuff that works in the real world. From hacking embedded systems to launching an exam scheduling platform as my final project, I learned how to turn ideas into solutions that deliver.

The Hustle in Action

My career kicked off at a situational command and control (SCC) startup, where I built software that had to work—no excuses. One standout? Echo—a satellite device I developed for special forces, enabling secure beyond-line-of-sight comms. Integrated with the Track24 SCC TITAN system, it snagged a U.S. patent and proved tech can be a lifeline when it’s done right.

Prior to that I ported an AES encryption module to LUA, clinching a high-stakes Saudi deal. That wasn’t just a coding win, it was a business save, showing I can bridge the gap between tech headaches and bottom-line results.

In 2019, I joined Contextere to lead an AI platform for blue-collar workers. The mission: deliver AI in real life context. It had to solve real problems and deliver value where it counts—on the ground.

As a Microsoft Certified Azure Solutions Architect Expert, I architect cloud solutions that are secure, scalable, and tough as nails. I’ve also deployed apps across AWS, Google Cloud, and DigitalOcean, squeezing every ounce of performance and savings out of cloud-native tech.

What I’m Building Now

Today, I’m all about scalable AI that tackles real pain points. My latest hustle, SynTextAI, uses AI to understand documents, pulling out insights that drive smarter decisions and unlock potential others miss.
